Privacy Policy

Effective Date: June 02, 2026  ·  Version 1.0

Short version: X Likes Gallery operates entirely on your device. We do not collect, store, or transmit your personal data to any external server. Everything stays in your browser.

1. Overview

X Likes Gallery ("the Extension") is a Chrome browser extension that allows you to view and organize media (images and videos) from posts you have liked on X (formerly Twitter) in a Pinterest-style gallery.

This Privacy Policy explains what data the Extension accesses, how it is used, and your rights regarding that data. By installing or using the Extension, you agree to the practices described in this policy.

2. Data We Access

The Extension accesses the following data solely to provide its core functionality:

Data Purpose Storage
Media URLs
(image & video URLs from liked posts)		 Display media in the gallery Local only
Post (Tweet) URLs Link back to the original post Local only
Author handles
(e.g. @username)		 Display attribution; enable author filtering Local only
User-defined tags & bookmarks Organize and filter your gallery Local only
X CSRF token (ct0) Authenticate requests to X's API to fetch post metadata. Never stored — held in memory only for the duration of the browser session. Memory only

What we do NOT access

  • Your X (Twitter) login credentials or password
  • Your direct messages, drafts, or private account data
  • Posts you have not liked
  • Any data from websites other than x.com and twitter.com
  • Your browsing history outside of x.com / twitter.com

3. How Data Is Stored

All data collected by the Extension is stored exclusively in Chrome's local extension storage (chrome.storage.local) on your own device. This means:

  • Data never leaves your browser.
  • We have no servers and receive no data from you.
  • Data persists only until you clear your extension data or uninstall the Extension.
  • The CSRF token (ct0) is held in memory only and is never written to persistent storage.

4. Permissions Explained

The Extension requests the following Chrome permissions:

  • storage / unlimitedStorage — Required to save your gallery data, tags, and settings locally on your device.
  • tabs — Required to detect the active tab and open the full-screen dashboard. The Extension does not read the URLs of tabs outside of x.com / twitter.com.
  • Host permissions for x.com, twitter.com, pbs.twimg.com, and related domains — Required to read liked-post content and load media from X's CDN.

5. Third-Party Services

The Extension interacts with X's internal GraphQL API (x.com/i/api/graphql/…) to fetch post metadata for media items imported from your X data archive. This interaction occurs using your existing authenticated browser session and is subject to X's Privacy Policy.

We do not share data with any analytics providers, advertisers, or other third parties.

6. Data Retention & Deletion

Because all data is stored locally on your device, you are in full control:

  • Delete all data: Uninstall the Extension from Chrome. All stored data is immediately and permanently removed.
  • Delete partial data: Use the "Clear Data" option within the Extension's dashboard (if available), or clear Chrome extension storage manually via chrome://settings/content/all.

We have no ability to access or delete data on your behalf because we never receive it.

7. Children's Privacy

The Extension is not directed at children under the age of 13 (or the applicable age in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect data from children. If you believe a child has used this Extension, please uninstall it from the device.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Effective Date" at the top of this page and update the Extension version in the Chrome Web Store. Continued use of the Extension after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Significant changes that affect data handling practices will be communicated through the Extension's Chrome Web Store listing or update notes.
